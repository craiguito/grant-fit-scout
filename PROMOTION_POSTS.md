## Post 1 - Grant Writers / Proposal Consultants

Title:

I made a Grants.gov shortlist workflow for deciding what is worth reading first

Body:

I kept running into the same problem with Grants.gov searches: the raw results are easy to get, but the first-pass decision is still tedious. Which ones are actually worth opening? Which have enough runway? Which have eligibility clues? Which look like a heavy proposal lift?

I put together a small workflow that scores public Grants.gov opportunities and turns them into a triage table with deadline urgency, fit reasons, workload, award notes, eligibility notes, contacts, and source links.

Here is a sample output:

https://craiguito.github.io/grant-fit-scout/sample-output.html

The paid version runs on Apify if someone wants repeatable CSV exports:

https://apify.com/nimble_flash/grant-fit-scout

I would be interested in feedback from grant writers: what fields would actually make this useful before opening the full NOFO?

## Post 2 - Community College / Workforce Angle

Title:

Community college workforce grant shortlist from Grants.gov

Body:

I built a small Grants.gov triage workflow for community colleges and workforce programs.

The point is not to replace NOFO review. It is to get from broad search terms like "workforce development", "technical education", or "cybersecurity education" to a short list with:

- fit score
- days to close
- urgency
- workload estimate
- award clues
- eligibility notes when available
- direct Grants.gov links

Community college landing page:

https://craiguito.github.io/grant-fit-scout/community-college-grants.html

Sample report:

https://craiguito.github.io/grant-fit-scout/sample-output.html
